Class Feedback

Current Situation

In academic institutions, every semester, a group of students take new courses based on their curriculum and degree. Students who enroll in their classes for a new semester face new challenges and hurdles while acquiring knowledge in terms of assignments, exams, projects, classes, etc. After each semester the students acquire experience based on their performance which gives them the ability to put into perspective how difficult and what strategies are best to pass a course. Meanwhile, other students are confused about whether to take a class or not and rely on the knowledge, based on other students to be able to perform better and be prepared for their semester. Most of the time students will base their decision mostly on feedback from other students that have previously taken the course.


Solution

Create a platform where people that have been verified to be students at the university can comment on a course with their experiences. They can write about unofficial pre-requisites, materials that will need to be purchased, topics that are discussed in class, and any other relevant information they would like to share. This feedback can be accessed by other people, whether they are students or not.


How it works

Students who are enrolled at the University of Puerto Rico Mayaguez will have access to create an account on our application. The sign-in process allows the user to input, at least, an email account registered within the UPR database and a password of their choice. Therefore, the student will become a member and will acquire access to post comments on the selected course. Comments will be stored in the system and are going to be shown to any user. To be able to have access to write or read a comment on the course the student will select a course he decides to comment on by writing the course code, course name, and department. The application allows users to write comments anonymously or not. Also, students will be able to vote and give feedback to comments. The comments will be prioritized based on the user feedback and votes. Students will also have access to search for the most recent comments or votes.


Goals:

1. Reduce frustration and burn-out in students

2. Reduce the number of dropouts and transfers due to a misunderstanding of what the career involves. This also improves the availability of courses for the people that need them.

3. Allow professors to focus on the topics of the class and not have to go backwards to topics in pre-requisites. This improves the quality of the education overall.
